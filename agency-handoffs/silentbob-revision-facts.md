## 4. PHOTO/SOURCE HONESTY RULES

Simple rules for every lane:

1. A photo's origin must be trackable. If you cannot say where it came from and confirm its category, do not use it.
2. Alt text must describe the image, not make a claim about the business. Alt text is crawled by Google. Lying in alt text is SEO fraud and a brand liability.
3. The photo inventory is the record of truth for assets. Not the Figma. Not a Slack message. The inventory file.
4. If Toby supplies real photos mid-revision, Silent Bob must be notified, the photos logged as Category A, and the relevant page captions updated before redeployment.
5. No photo from a competitor's site, a manufacturer's press kit, or a homeowner's own project may be used without explicit licensing. When in doubt, use Unsplash/Pexels trade-licensed stock or AI-generated Category B.
6. If a photo source is unknown — even if it "came with the template" or "was already in the repo" — classify it Category B and log it. Do not assume it is Category A.

## 5. SERVICE-AREA LOCAL SEO BOUNDARY

The revision calls for roughly 1000-1200 customer-facing words per city page. This section defines what that copy may and may not contain.

### 5a. What is allowed on city pages

- Accurate description of JAG Electric services as confirmed (panel upgrades, rewiring, lighting, outlets/switches, remodel/addition, new construction, commercial, computer cable).
- Honest framing of geographic reach: "serving [City] and the surrounding area," "homeowners and businesses in [City]," "JAG Electric makes regular service calls to [City] from our base in Galena, KS."
- Publicly verifiable local context: city name, county name, state, general region ("Southeast Kansas," "the Tri-State area" for Joplin). These are factual and safe.
- Real reasons a homeowner in that city would call an electrician: aging wiring in older homes, panel upgrade needs for EV chargers, code compliance before a home sale, kitchen remodel electrical, etc. These are category-level truths, not JAG-specific claims.
- Internal links to relevant service pages.
- Phone CTA and estimate-prep link.
- Approved review snippets where relevant.

### 5b. What is NOT allowed on city pages

- Invented local landmarks tied to JAG ("near the [City] downtown square, we've completed dozens of rewires"). Unless a project in that city is confirmed, do not imply a project history there.
- City-specific project counts, permit counts, or job volume claims. None are confirmed.
- Named local partners, contractors, or referral sources in that city. None are confirmed.
- Claims that JAG has a local crew, local office, or local yard in that city. They do not. Galena is the business address.
- MO licensing claims on the Joplin page. KS electrical contractor license has not been confirmed in either state. Joplin copy must say "serving customers in the Joplin, MO area" without any licensing assertion.
- Population statistics, median home values, or local market data presented as JAG's knowledge of the area rather than publicly sourced context. If you use a census stat, it must read as public context, not a business claim.

### 5c. City-by-city local SEO notes

Pittsburg, KS (primary market):
- Anchor city per GBP and Toby. Most local SEO weight should land here.
- Safe framing: "our primary service market," "the Pittsburg area," "Crawford County."
- Pittsburg is the largest city in Crawford County with an active residential and commercial base. That is publicly verifiable and safe to use as context.

Galena, KS (business address city):
- This is where JAG is actually based. The page can state that directly.
- Safe framing: "located in Galena, KS," "our home base," "Cherokee County."
- Do not overstate Galena as a separate major market — it is a small town. The page earns its value from being the actual address city.

Joplin, MO (across state line):
- Omit all licensing language. No KS or MO license number or claim.
- Safe framing: "serving the Joplin, MO area," "the Joplin metro," "the Tri-State area."
- Note the Joplin page's existence on the sitemap as a geographic reach signal, but do not overclaim depth of presence there.

Frontenac, KS:
- Small city adjacent to Pittsburg in Crawford County. Safe to note proximity.
- Safe framing: "serving Frontenac and the greater Pittsburg area," "Crawford County."

Girard, KS:
- Crawford County seat. Safe to note county connection.
- Safe framing: "serving Girard and the surrounding Crawford County communities."

### 5d. Schema boundary

LocalBusiness schema must use the confirmed business address (807 E 7th St, Galena, KS 66739) and confirmed phone number only.
