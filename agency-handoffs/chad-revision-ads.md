## Measurement plan draft

No launch until tracking is known. Required measurement checks:

- Phone click event.
- Email click event.
- Estimate-prep start event.
- Estimate-prep submit/complete event if the tool exists and is honest.
- Service-page view events.
- Meta landing page view if pixel exists.
- Google Ads call extension/call asset tracking if approved.
- UTM structure for every draft ad.

Draft UTM convention:

- utm_source=google or meta
- utm_medium=cpc or paid_social
- utm_campaign=jag_electric_service_test
- utm_content=[angle]_[format]_[variant]
- utm_term=[keyword_theme] for Google Search only

Example:

/services/panel-upgrades?utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=jag_electric_service_test&utm_content=panel_search_rsa_a&utm_term=panel_upgrade

## Readout cadence draft

Only after authorized launch:

- First 24–48 hours: check spend pacing, disapprovals, click quality, broken URLs, phone/email/prep events.
- Day 3–5: read early CTR, CPC, search terms, location quality, landing-page behavior.
- Day 7: first decision point. Pause waste, keep clean search terms, fork best hooks.
- Day 14: compare service angles. Shift budget only toward evidence.
- Day 30: decide scale, rebuild, or stop based on qualified contacts and actual local demand signals.

## Decision rules draft

Scale only if:

- Tracking is proven.
- Search terms or Meta engagement are local and service-relevant.
- Cost per qualified action is acceptable to Toby.
- Landing-page behavior supports the service angle.
- Creative is not fatigue-heavy.

Pause if:

- Search terms drift.
- Geography leaks.
- CTR is weak across multiple variants.
- Page actions do not happen after enough traffic.
- Comments show mismatch or confusion.

Fork if:

- One service angle gets strong engagement but low conversion.
- One headline hook wins but landing page is too thin.
- One city page gets better action quality than the broader service area.

Kill if:

- The angle attracts DIY, price-shopping only, job seekers, supply shoppers, or outside-area traffic.
- The required landing page cannot support the claim or service depth.
- Visuals are too generic or claim-risky.

## Final approval gate before any spend

Before launch, Toby must approve:

- Platform(s).
- Daily budget and total test cap.
- Service geography.
- Final ad copy.
- Final visuals and their source labels.
- Landing pages.
- Tracking proof.
- Call/email/form handling path.
- Account access and exact campaign mutation scope.

Until that approval happens, this remains a draft-only media/ad package.
