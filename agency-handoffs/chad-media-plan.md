## Working hypotheses

H1 — Trust-first local electrician demand:
- Homeowners who need panel, wiring, outlet, fixture, or remodel electrical work will respond best to a trust-first message backed by verified reputation signals.
- Proof available: 4.9 Google rating with 50 reviews supplied by Toby, 100% Facebook recommendation based on 20 reviews, BBB A+ accredited, HomeAdvisor 4.9 from 45 reviews.
- Win condition before scaling: qualified calls or estimate-prep submissions at a sustainable cost, with project type and service area captured.

H2 — Panel upgrades and remodel/new-construction jobs produce higher-intent leads than broad “electrician near me” messaging:
- Panel upgrades, wiring, remodels, new construction, and commercial service are more concrete needs than generic electrician awareness.
- Test service-specific campaigns before broad local branding spend.
- Win condition: higher conversion rate and clearer project-fit notes than broad local ads.

H3 — Pittsburg-area homeowners need a low-friction path from “I have an electrical issue” to “I know what to ask when I call”:
- The estimate-prep page can reduce friction if positioned as a fast call-prep tool, not a fake automated quote engine.
- Win condition: users submit enough detail for JAG to prioritize follow-up, or click-to-call from the estimate-prep path.

H4 — Premium electrical visual identity can cut through local contractor sameness:
- The dark graphite / electric cyan / copper conductor direction should be used in creative to make JAG memorable without cheesy lightning gimmicks.
- Win condition: above-baseline CTR and click quality versus plain stock-contractor creative.
